Overview

Pakistan's first cinematic projection show on a national monument

On 14th August 2025, Pakistan's Independence Day, the iconic Mazar-e-Quaid in Karachi was transformed into a canvas of light. This was the country's first-ever animated cinematic laser projection mapping event at a national monument.

I was selected to contribute as the lead animation and cinematic visual designer, responsible for creating 4 full minutes of the 8-minute show — exactly half of the entire production.

My Role

What I created

01

Cinematic Animation

Designed and animated full cinematic sequences including the Quaid-e-Azam character animation, patriotic motion scenes, and historical narrative visuals.

02

VFX & Motion Graphics

Created VFX-driven sequences with glowing effects, particle systems, light trails, and cinematic color grading optimized for large-scale projection.

03

Projection Mapping

Precisely mapped all visual content to the 3D architecture of the Mazar-e-Quaid monument, ensuring perfect alignment across the building's surfaces.
